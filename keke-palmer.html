Career

Keke Palmer has been singing since she was a child, but her acting debut came in 2004 with the film “Barbershop 2: Back in Business.” She played Queen Latifah’s character’s niece. Six weeks after relocating to Los Angeles, she landed a role on CBS’s ‘Cold Case’ (2004). Within a year, she has been cast in a national K-Mart commercial and a nationwide search for a role opposite William H. Macy in the film “The Wool Cap.”

She auditioned for the ‘American Idol’ spin-off series ‘American Juniors,’ which was later canceled, in an attempt to establish herself as a singer during this time period. In 2005, she signed with Atlantic Records. Additionally, she starred in the 2005 television film ‘Knights of the South Bronx.’ In the same year, she appeared as a guest on three television shows: “Second Time Around,” “ER,” and “Law & Order: Special Victims Unit.”

In 2006, she was cast as ‘Akeelah Anderson’ in the award-winning film ‘Akeelah and the Bee.’ In the same year, she appeared as a supporting character in the film ‘Madea’s Family Reunion. The following year, her song ‘Tonight’ was used as the opening song for the film ‘Night at the Museum.
In 2007, she made an appearance on the Disney channel’s ‘Jump In!’, which featured her two songs ‘It’s My Turn Now’ and ‘Jumpin’. She also starred alongside Samuel Jackson and Eva Mendes in the thriller ‘Cleaner.’ Her debut album, ‘So Uncool,’ was released on September 18, 2007, and included the single ‘Footworkin’. Despite favorable reviews, the album failed to chart on Billboard.

In 2008, she starred as the lead in Nickelodeon’s ‘True Jackson, VP’. Palmer also sang the theme song for the show. In the same year, she starred in the films ‘The Longshots’ and ‘Shrink’. In 2010, she switched from Atlantic Records to Interscope Records as her record label. The following year, she released her self-titled debut mixtape, ‘Awaken.’ In 2012, she self-released her second mixtape.

From 2011 to 2014, she was the lead vocalist on Nickelodeon’s ‘Winx Club’. She also provided the voice of Peaches in the animated film ‘Ice Age: Continental Drift,’ which was released in 2012. She co-starred in the film ‘Joyful Noise’ alongside Queen Latifah and produced and starred in the Nickelodeon musical ‘Rags’ the same year. One of her most recent notable performances was in ‘Scream Queens,’ a Fox horror series.

Keke Palmer Husband, Marriage
